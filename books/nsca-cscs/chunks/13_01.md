Reasons for Testing
Testing helps athletes and coaches objectiv ely assess athletic talent
and identi fy physical abilities and areas in need of improvement. In
addition, test scores can be used in goal setting by setting attainable
benchmarks for athletes to aim for. Baseline measur ements can be
used to establ ish starting points against which achiev able training
goals can be set, and testing at regular interv als can help track an
athlete’ s progress in reaching those goals and provide insight into
fatigue, readiness, and injury risk. Using tests as a basis for goal
setting allows coaches to set specific goals for individual athletes
that, when taken together , help to accompl ish group or team
objectiv es (see chapter 9  for mor e information about goal set ting).
Assessment of Athletic Talent
It is important for a coach to determine whether an individual has
the physical potential to play a sport at the competi tive level of the
team. That judgment is not difficult if the candidate has already
excelled at the sport elsew here and is of adequate body size.
Howev er, in many cases, candidates have not clearly demonstr ated
their competi tive abilities or may lack experience in the sport. The
coach then needs some way of determining whether the candidate
has the necessary basic physical abilities that, in combination with
technique training and practice, could produce a competi tive player.
Field tests serve as tools for such assessment, and this application of
testing is r eferred to as talent identification .

Evidence-Based Testing
Evidence-based testing is essential, because it ensur es that training programs are
based on scienti fic resear ch and empirical evidence, which are both equally
important. S ome of the k ey reasons f or this f ollow:
Validity and reliability . Evidence-based testing provides a framework for
selecting tests that are both valid and reliable. By using validated and reliable
tests, practitioners can confidently assess an athlete’ s capabi lities and track
progress.
Individualization . Every athlete has distinct strengths, weaknesses, and
goals. Evidence-based testing allows practitioners to modify training
programs to the specific needs of each athlete. By conducting assessments
based on scienti fic evidence, strength and condi tioning professionals can
identi fy areas in need of improvement and develop targeted interv entions to
optimiz e perf ormance.
Injury prevention . Evidence-based testing helps identi fy biomechanical ly
inefficient movement patterns and muscular imbalances that may predispose
athletes to injury . By addressing these issues through targeted training
interv entions, strength and condi tioning professionals can reduce the risk of
injury and enhance athletic longevi ty.
Performance optimization . Evidence-based testing enables strength and
condi tioning professionals to identi fy performance-l imiting factors and
develop strategies to optimiz e athletic performance. By assessing key
physiological parameters such as strength, power , agility, and aerobic
endur ance, strength and condi tioning professionals can design training
programs that target specific areas for improvement, leading to enhanced
athletic perf ormance.
Monitoring prog ress. Regular assessmen t is essential for monitoring
progress and evaluating the effectiveness of training interv entions. Evidence-
based testing provides objectiv e measur es of performance that allows
strength and condi tioning professionals to track changes over time and make
data-driv en decisions regarding program modifications. This ensur es that
training pr ograms r emain al igned wi th perf ormance goals.

Screening  for Fatigue and Injury Risk
Monitoring acute and chronic fatigue due to training and competi tion
exposur es and associated injury risk factors can allow the strength
and condi tioning profession al to prospectiv ely modula te training
programs to promote athlete health and performance. Although both
fatigue and injury risk mechanisms are complex and multifaceted,
combining quanti tative and qualitative tests can provide valuable
insights for the strength and condi tioning professional and inform
any requirements for acute adjustments to predetermined training
programs, particularly during the competi tion period.
